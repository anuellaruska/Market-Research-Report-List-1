<p><ul><li>Biochemical Transfection</li><li>Physical Transfection</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The transfection reagent market is divided into two main types: biochemical transfection and physical transfection. Biochemical transfection involves the use of chemical agents, like lipids or polymers, to facilitate the delivery of nucleic acids into cells, ensuring high efficiency and low cytotoxicity. In contrast, physical transfection employs methods such as electroporation or microinjection, where physical forces are applied to introduce genetic material directly into cells. Both approaches are essential for research and therapeutic applications in gene editing and molecular biology.</p></p>
